# Behaviour Logging Guide

The most powerful feature in Corpus isn't a metric — it's the connection between what you *do* and how your body *responds*. Behaviour logging is how you build that connection.

---

## Why Logging Matters

You probably have hunches. Maybe late coffee ruins your sleep. Maybe alcohol tanks your recovery. Maybe meditation actually helps. But hunches aren't proof.

Corpus turns hunches into evidence. When you log your daily habits — food, drinks, supplements, recovery activities, stress levels — Corpus analyzes them against your health outcomes over time and surfaces what actually matters for *your* body.

The catch? It needs the data. The more consistently you log, the faster and more accurate the insights become.

---

## Five Ways to Log

### 1. Morning Check-In

Three quick questions each morning about yesterday. Tap yes or no, pick from a few options, and you're done in under 10 seconds. The questions adapt over time — Corpus asks about the things that matter most for your body.

### 2. Quick-Tap Tags

On your dashboard, you'll see suggestion chips based on your recent logging habits. Had a late meal? Took magnesium? Tap the tag. One touch, logged.

### 3. Notes

Type a quick note anytime. "Had a huge steak dinner at 9pm" or "Feeling anxious all day." Corpus reads your notes and automatically extracts relevant behaviour tags — you don't need to be precise or use specific terms. Just write naturally.

### 4. Photo Logging

Snap a photo of your meal, your supplement stack, or anything relevant. Corpus analyzes the image, generates a description, and extracts behaviour tags automatically. It's the fastest way to log food — no typing, no calorie counting, just a photo.

### 5. Automatic Detection

Some behaviours are logged for you. Corpus detects naps, meditation sessions, stretching and yoga workouts, and menstrual cycle data directly from Apple Health — no manual input needed.

## What You Can Track

Corpus covers a wide range of daily behaviours across several categories:

**Food & Drink** — Late meals, heavy meals, high sugar, high protein, hydration, fasting, home-cooked food, fast food, and more. No calorie counting — just what you ate and when.

**Substances** — Alcohol, late caffeine, cannabis, nicotine. Both presence and intensity (e.g. heavy drinking vs. a glass of wine).

**Sleep Habits** — Late screen time, sleep mask use, bedroom temperature, naps.

**Recovery** — Meditation, breathwork, stretching, massage, cold exposure, sauna, foam rolling.

**Supplements** — Magnesium, creatine, melatonin, vitamin D, omega-3.

**Stress** — Work stress and life stress, each on a simple Low / Moderate / High scale.

**Symptoms** — Stomach issues, headache, low energy, muscle soreness, anxiety, brain fog, skin breakout, joint pain. These work both as things to track *and* as outcomes — Corpus can discover what *causes* your symptoms.

## How Correlations Work

Here's the magic. After you've been logging for a couple of weeks, Corpus starts comparing days when you did something versus days when you didn't.

For example: on the 12 days you had late caffeine, your average deep sleep was 28% lower than on the 30 days you didn't. That's not a generic health tip — that's *your* data telling *your* story.

Corpus surfaces these as personalized insights:

- *"Late screens are associated with poorer sleep quality for you"*
- *"Cold exposure days show better next-day recovery"*
- *"High work stress is associated with 1.8x higher anxiety the next day"*

The system also detects **delayed effects** — a behaviour today can affect how you feel tomorrow. Dairy tonight might mean stomach issues in the morning. A stressful day at work might show up as poor sleep that night and low readiness the next day. Corpus tracks these day-over-day patterns automatically.

## Tips for Getting Started

**Consistency beats perfection.** You don't need to log every single thing. Start with the check-in each morning and a couple of photo logs at meals. That's enough to get started.

**Use photos for food.** It's faster than typing and captures more detail. Just snap and move on — Corpus handles the rest.

**Don't overthink it.** A note that says "felt stressed" works just as well as a detailed entry. Corpus extracts what it needs.

**Give it two to three weeks.** The correlation engine needs enough data points to find meaningful patterns. Fourteen days of consistent logging is usually when the first insights appear.

**Check your insights regularly.** Head to your Profile to see all active insights — you might be surprised by what your data reveals. Some of the most valuable discoveries are things you never would have suspected.

**Log symptoms too.** Tracking headaches, low energy, or stomach issues helps Corpus find their triggers. You might discover a food sensitivity or stress pattern you didn't know about.

The bottom line: a few seconds of logging each day compounds into genuinely personal health intelligence over time. Your body has patterns. Corpus helps you see them.
