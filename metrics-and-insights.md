# Metrics & AI Insights

Corpus distills the flood of data from your Apple Watch and iPhone into six clear health metrics — plus body composition if you use a smart scale. Each metric has distinct levels so you always know where you stand, not just a number.

---

## The Six Core Metrics

### Sleep

Your sleep quality is assessed across multiple dimensions — not just how long you were in bed.

- **Levels:** Excellent · Good · Fair · Poor
- **What's tracked:** Total duration, time in deep and REM stages, interruptions, regularity, and how your sleep compares to your personalized target.
- **Sleep debt:** Corpus tracks cumulative sleep debt so you can see whether you're catching up or falling behind.
- **Nap detection:** Daytime naps are automatically identified and counted separately from nighttime sleep.
- **What to do:** Fair or Poor? Look at your recent habits — Corpus may already have an insight about what's disrupting your sleep. Check your sleep detail view for a 30-day schedule chart showing your bedtimes, wake times, and patterns.

### Readiness

A daily snapshot of how prepared your body is for physical and mental demands.

- **Levels:** Optimal · Good · Moderate · Low · Very Low
- **What it reflects:** Your resting heart rate, heart rate variability, recent sleep quality, recovery status, and training load all feed into this metric.
- **What to do:** Optimal or Good — push yourself. Moderate — listen to your body. Low or Very Low — prioritize recovery; your coach will adjust your plan accordingly.

### Strain

How much cumulative physical load your body has absorbed.

- **Levels:** Minimal · Light · Moderate · High · Maximal
- **What it reflects:** Workout intensity, duration, and frequency combined with your daily activity.
- **Training load tracking:** Corpus monitors your acute (7-day) versus chronic (28-day) workload to flag when you're ramping up too fast or not training enough to meet your goals.
- **What to do:** High or Maximal strain with Low readiness? That's a signal to ease off. Minimal strain when you're feeling Good? Time to move.

### Stress

Your body's physiological stress response — not just how you feel, but what your biometrics reveal.

- **Levels:** Relaxed · Mild · Elevated · High
- **What it reflects:** Heart rate variability patterns, resting heart rate trends, and other autonomic signals.
- **What to do:** Elevated or High stress doesn't always mean you feel stressed — it could be physical overtraining, poor sleep, or illness. Check your logged behaviours for clues, or ask your AI coach.

### Recovery

How well your body is bouncing back from strain and stress.

- **Levels:** Fully Recovered · Recovering · Underrecovered · Depleted
- **What it reflects:** The interplay of sleep quality, strain, stress, and heart rate variability trends over recent days.
- **What to do:** Depleted? Your weekly plan will reflect this. Recovering? A light session might actually help. Fully Recovered? You're green-lit to go hard.

### Steps

Your daily step count with context — not just a number, but a trend.

- **What's tracked:** Today's count, a 7-day daily breakdown, and your 30-day average.
- **What to do:** Steps are a simple but powerful proxy for general movement. Corpus shows you the trend so you can see if you're becoming more or less active over time.

## Body Composition

If you use a smart scale that syncs with Apple Health, Corpus brings your body composition data into the picture.

- **What's tracked:** Weight, body fat percentage, and lean body mass.
- **Trend charts:** 90-day scatter and line charts for weight, fat percentage, and lean mass show you the real trajectory — not day-to-day noise.
- **Derived metrics:** Estimated basal metabolic rate (BMR), total daily energy expenditure (TDEE), and energy balance.
- **Recomposition detection:** When your fat percentage is dropping while your weight stays stable, Corpus highlights it — you're building muscle and losing fat simultaneously. That's progress a scale alone would miss.
- **Freshness:** Body composition data only appears when your most recent reading is within 14 days, so you're always looking at current data.

## AI Insights

The metrics tell you *where you are*. The AI insight system tells you *why* — and what to do about it.

### Daily Coaching

Every morning, Corpus generates a personalized coaching comment — two to four sentences about your current state, what happened overnight, and what to focus on today. It's written with your goals, your recent history, and your recovery in mind.

### Morning Check-In

Three quick questions about yesterday's habits — tailored to you. The questions adapt based on what Corpus has learned matters most for your body. Early on they cover common factors like caffeine, alcohol, and screen time. As patterns emerge, they zero in on your personal triggers.

### Personalized Insights

This is where Corpus gets truly personal. By analyzing your logged behaviours against your health outcomes over a 90-day window, Corpus discovers statistically significant patterns unique to you:

- *"Late caffeine is associated with 28% less deep sleep"*
- *"Meditation days show higher next-day readiness"*
- *"Dairy is associated with 2.1× higher stomach issues"*

These aren't generic tips — they're evidence from your own data.

### Weekly Training Plans

Your AI coach builds a structured weekly plan aligned to your goals, fitness level, schedule, and current recovery status. Plans adapt when life gets in the way — mark a day as busy, and the plan reshuffles.

### Post-Workout Commentary

After each workout (over two minutes), Corpus generates a short insight about your session — how your heart rate responded, how the effort compares to recent sessions, and what it means for your recovery.

### Chat Coach

Your coach is always available. Ask anything: "Should I run today?", "Why is my readiness low?", "Help me plan for my race next month." It has full context on your data, your goals, and your history.

### Gets Smarter Over Time

Every day you use Corpus, the picture gets sharper. The AI coach refines its understanding of your body, your habits, your patterns, and your responses. Check-in questions become more targeted. Insights become more specific. Plans become more attuned to what works for you.

The first few days give you solid metrics and coaching. After two to three weeks of consistent logging, the personalized insights start appearing. After a few months, Corpus knows your body in ways that would take a human coach years to learn.
